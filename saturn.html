Saturn

Saturn's overwhelming observational attraction is its stunning system of rings. From Earth, three distinct, major rings can be seen encircling Saturn's equator. As a gas giant, Saturn doesn't have a true surface. The planet is mostly swirling gases and liquids deeper down. While a spacecraft would have nowhere to land on Saturn, it wouldn't be able to fly through unscathed either.
